Point set topology (or General topology) deals with the basic set-theoretic definitions and constructions used in topology. It is the foundation of most other branches of topology, including differential topology, geometric topology, and algebraic topology.

The notions fundamental to point-set topology are continuity, compactness, and connectedness.

To build those we need notions of open sets and closed sets in \(R^n\). We use open ball and closed ball as their respective examples.

[Figure Note that U of open sets in R is open. Finite intersection of open sets in Rn]

A collection of open sets is a topological space if they satisfy the following conditions:

  • M and empty set are open.
  • U and V being empty sets => U intersection V is open.
  • Union of any collection of open sets is open.

Neighborhood and Continuous Maps

Any open set in M containing a point \(x \in M\) is a neighborhood of \(x\).

If \(F:M\rightarrow N\) then \(F\) is continuous if for every open set \(V\subset U\), the inverse image \(F^{-1}V := {x \in M | F(x) \in V}\) is open in \(M\).
